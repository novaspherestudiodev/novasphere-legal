01 The Philosophy of Data Sovereignty

NovaBiz OS operates on a strict Offline-First Cryptographic Architecture. We believe that your business data belongs to you. Unlike cloud-dependent SaaS platforms that harvest and analyze your financial ledgers and client data on remote servers, NovaBiz OS confines your operational data strictly to your physical hardware.

NovaSphere Studio acts as the architect of the software, but You (The Enterprise) remain the sole Data Controller of all information processed within the matrix.

02 Data Confinement (What We Do Not Collect)

NovaSphere Studio has zero access to your internal operations. We DO NOT collect, transmit, or monitor:

  • Entity DNA (CRM): Your client names, phone numbers, loyalty tiers, and metadata.
  • Financial Ledgers: Your revenue, transaction histories, invoices, and pricing configurations.
  • Operational Directives: Staff shift schedules, physical inventory levels, and internal tasks.
  • Biometric Pins: Your staff access codes and Master Boss PINs.
  • Encrypted Documents: ID scans and contracts uploaded into the Personnel Uplink.

All of the above is encrypted and mathematically sealed inside the Local Isar Database Vault located on your physical hard drive.

03 System Telemetry (What We Do Collect)

To ensure the OS functions securely, NovaSphere Studio only communicates with the software for vital system health checks:

  • License Authentication: When you renew your Kernel License, the system transmits a digital handshake (containing your PC's unique hardware identifier) to verify payment validity via our secure gateway (Paystack).
  • Version Pinging: The OS routinely checks the NovaSphere Global GitHub Servers to verify if you are running a compromised executable or if a critical security patch is available.
  • Crash Diagnostics (Optional): If you utilize the NovaSphere Uplink to manually report an anomaly, the diagnostic data you choose to send via email will be collected to engineer a patch.

Why: To establish the Hidden Cryptographic Vault (AppData / Application Support) where your database is safely buried. This permission is also used to import/crop images (Staff Avatars, Asset Photos, Documents) from your PC, and to export PDF financial ledgers to your hard drive.
B. Network & Internet Communications
Why: To execute secure Paystack license renewals, ping the NovaSphere servers for critical updates, and route automated messages (WhatsApp/SMS) from the Comms Boss out to your clients.
C. Hardware/Device Fingerprinting
Why: To generate a unique cryptographic signature bound to your motherboard. This prevents software piracy, enforces the 14-Day Free Trial countdown, and ensures your license key cannot be cloned to another facility.
D. Spooler & USB / Printer Interfacing
Why: To autonomously route digital invoices directly to external hardware, such as 80mm Thermal POS receipt printers or standard A4 desk printers.
